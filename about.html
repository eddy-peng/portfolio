About

Hello there, I'm Eddy!

I am a product designer who thrives in fast-paced, collaborative teams through innovation and storytelling to stay ahead of design and business demands.

I majored in Informatics (HCI) at the University of Washington in Seattle, graduating with the Summa Cum Laude distinction (0.5% of the 2024 graduating class).

My frequent exposure to fast-paced working environments and projects made me adopt a mindset of learning and applying knowledge rapidly to solve complex problems.

Those environments also allowed me to wear many hats and work on multiple projects concurrently.

Industries

I designed for companies and organizations from a wide range of industries.

I've built B2B and B2C products (sometimes from 0 to 1) at companies and organizations from industries like finance, education, entertainment, consulting, energy and utilities, mental health, artificial intelligence, cybersecurity, and healthcare (to name a few).

Process

I take a business-oriented and agile approach to problem-solving.

I follow a general agile framework in all my projects, utilizing additional methodologies when needed to structure my thinking and solve complex problems. When I start new projects, there tends to be a lot of information to grasp. Understanding the context, like the people involved and the business needs, is essential and influences my design process.

Why UX?

Before beginning my User Experience career, I was a writer.

I enjoyed writing stories and essays because I loved the process, which included brainstorming, drafting, getting feedback, and iterating. My love of writing led me to Product Design, where I continued to fulfill my enjoyment of improving something through a process, being creative, and telling stories while mixing in the problem-solving and business aspects.

Philosophy

I like playing the long-term game.

No matter where I am in my design journey, I believe there is always room for growth. My philosophy also spreads to the products I have designed. With the knowledge that nothing will finalized, I must strive towards the best version and be confident in its success going into the future.

Tools

I use a range of design and engineering technologies.

I use Figma for designing and FigJam for whiteboarding. However, I prefer Miro when collaborating with stakeholders who are non-designers. I also use engineering tools to illustrate concepts to engineers or assist them with React.js or Next.js. Lastly, AI tools like ChatGPT allow me to adapt rapidly in a landscape with fluid business needs.
