About

Hello there, I'm Eddy!

I am a product designer who thrives in fast-paced, collaborative teams through innovation and storytelling to stay ahead of design and business demands.

I majored in Informatics (HCI) at the University of Washington in Seattle, graduating with the Summa Cum Laude distinction (0.5% of the 2024 graduating class).

My frequent exposure to fast-paced working environments and projects made me adopt a mindset of learning and applying knowledge rapidly to solve complex problems.

Those environments also allowed me to wear many hats and work on multiple projects concurrently.

Origin

My desire to optimize for people, passion for writing, and a chance encounter in a high school classroom set me on a journey.

Growing up, I noticed the people around me (mainly my family) changing their behaviors to optimize and achieve their objectives easier. Some simple examples include moving furniture to make it easier to traverse and moving frequently used items closer to eye level for quicker interactions.

Yet, there were some limits to what they could control. Occasionally, people around me would struggle to adapt to new technologies and software (like attempting to find information on a healthcare website in a moment of need). The frustrating moments planted a seed in me.

I got a step closer when I realized my love of storytelling. In much of my early education years, writing courses were my favorite. I enjoyed telling stories and synthesizing essays because I liked the creative process, which included brainstorming, drafting, getting feedback, and iterating.

Then, I took a high school visual design class that used the Adobe Software Suite and introduced Adobe XD. The user experience design floodgates were opened. I caught a glimpse into the future. I saw myself in a design role where I could optimize systems for people and apply my passion for storytelling. Thus, I set out on my adventure.
